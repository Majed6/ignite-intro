Today I would like to introduce you to Ignite talks.

What's an Ignite presentation?

Speakers have five minutes to talk on a subject accompanied by 20 slides, for 15 seconds each, automatically advanced.

What's an Ignite talk ?

It's a fast-paced event started in 2006 by Brady Forrest and Bre Pettis in Seattle. Ignite is also about having fun, and showing that presentations don’t need to be about “death by PowerPoint”.

What makes a good ignite talk?

It is good to breathe and have pauses.

Figure out your points before you make slides

Don’t get hung up on slides. What you say matters most.

Keep your fonts large. It makes the text easier to read for the audience.

Make your talk fault tolerant. Where each piece could stand alone.

Know Your Key Moments. Use Them as Anchors when recovering.

300 seconds is easy to practice. So practice.

And practice.

Don’t Stop. Practice Recovering. Learn to recover from mistakes.

Practice some more.

Plan to lose your first and last slide.

Hack the format. It's 20 slides 15 seconds each and that's the only restriction.

That's all. Enjoy the pictures.
